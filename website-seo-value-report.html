The Challenge: New Domain, Crowded Market

Home exterior services (roofing, siding, windows) in Connecticut is one of the most competitive local SEO landscapes. You're not just competing against other contractors — you're competing against:

  • National lead-gen platforms (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack) that dominate page 1 for nearly every "[service] near me" search
  • Established local competitors with 5-10+ year old domains, hundreds of reviews, and years of backlink authority
  • Franchise operations (Power Home Remodeling, Window World) with massive ad budgets and dedicated SEO teams

A brand-new domain starts at zero. No domain authority. No indexed pages. No backlinks. No search history. Google doesn't trust you yet — you have to earn it. That takes strategy, not shortcuts.

Why New Domains Face an Uphill Battle

Google's algorithm favors established sites. A competitor who's had their domain for 8 years with consistent content and backlinks has a compounding advantage. A new domain needs to:

  1. Get indexed — Google needs to discover and crawl every page
  2. Build topical authority — Prove you're a legitimate, comprehensive resource on your services
  3. Earn trust signals — Reviews, backlinks, citations, consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) data
  4. Demonstrate E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness

This isn't a 30-day process. Most SEO professionals estimate 6-12 months before a new domain in a competitive local market sees meaningful organic traffic. The work done in months 1-3 is foundational — it may not show dramatic ranking improvements immediately, but without it, rankings will never come.

Phase 1: Site Architecture & Content (Foundation)

21 Town-Specific Service Pages Created

Each page features unique, locally-targeted content (650-700 words) covering:

Why this matters: Each town page targets a specific long-tail keyword (e.g., "vinyl siding installation in Branford CT") that has lower competition than broad terms. This is how you win on a new domain — you don't go after "roofing CT" on day one. You build authority through the long tail and let it compound.

5 Service Hub Pages Optimized

  • Roofing, Siding, Windows & Doors, Gutters, Decks
  • Each with Rank Math SEO configuration (focus keywords, meta titles, meta descriptions)
  • Internal links to all relevant town pages
  • External links to authoritative sources (Energy Star, InterNACHI, Remodeling Magazine)

12 Blog Posts Written & Scheduled

Staggered publication over 4 weeks to appear natural to search engines:

  • 3 roofing-focused posts (with links to all 7 roofing town pages)
  • 3 siding-focused posts (with links to all 7 siding town pages)
  • 3 windows/doors-focused posts (with links to all 7 windows & doors town pages)
  • 3 general exterior posts (with links to multiple service hub pages)
Why this matters: Blog content targets informational search queries ("best roofing materials for CT homes") that service pages can't rank for, and the internal links pass authority to the service pages that actually convert visitors into leads.

Phase 2: Technical SEO

Rank Math Configuration (All Pages)

  • Focus keywords matched to URL slugs (eliminates "keyword not in URL" errors)
  • Custom SEO titles and meta descriptions for every service page
  • Rank Math scores: 60-74/100 across all configured pages
  • Basic SEO checks passing on all town pages

Schema Markup (26 Pages + Home)

  • LocalBusiness schema on home page (business name, address, phone, service area, hours)
  • Service schema on all 26 service pages (5 hubs + 21 town pages)
  • Each schema includes: service name, service type, description, provider info, area served
Why this matters: Schema markup is how you communicate directly with search engines in their language. It enables rich results (star ratings, business info, service details) in search listings, which dramatically improve click-through rates.

Custom Estimate Form

  • Mobile-responsive design
  • Formspree integration for email delivery
  • Branded to match site design (navy/red color scheme)

The SEO Roadmap: What Happens Next

The foundation is built. Here's what determines whether this investment pays off:

Immediate Priorities (Month 1)

  • Publish blog posts on schedule
  • Add content expansions to hub pages via Divi Visual Builder
  • Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
  • Verify all pages are indexed

Short-Term (Months 1-3)

  • Monitor Google Search Console for impressions and clicks
  • Build Google Business Profile citations with consistent NAP data
  • Begin collecting customer reviews (Google, Yelp, BBB)
  • Share blog posts on social media

Medium-Term (Months 3-6)

  • Expect initial rankings for long-tail town-specific keywords
  • Blog posts should begin appearing in informational searches
  • Analyze which pages are gaining traction and double down
  • Consider additional blog content targeting questions your customers ask

Long-Term (Months 6-12)

  • Topical authority compounds — hub pages start ranking for broader terms
  • Organic traffic grows as Google trusts the domain more
  • Internal link structure distributes authority across the site
  • Local pack visibility improves with reviews and citation consistency
