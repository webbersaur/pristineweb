Why Hamden Homes Need Window and Door Upgrades

Many Hamden homes were built in the 1950s through 1970s and still have their original windows—single-pane glass in aluminum or wooden frames that offer minimal insulation and poor air sealing. These outdated windows are responsible for significant heat loss in winter and unwanted heat gain in summer, driving up energy bills year-round.

The U.S. Department of Energy estimates that windows account for 25-30% of a home's heating and cooling energy use. For older Hamden homes with original single-pane windows, that number can be even higher. Upgrading to modern double-pane windows with Low-E glass and argon gas fill can dramatically reduce energy costs while eliminating cold spots, condensation, and drafts that make rooms uncomfortable during Connecticut's harsh winters.

New doors are equally impactful. A worn-out front door with gaps around the frame wastes energy and compromises security. Modern entry doors with insulated cores, tight-fitting frames, and multipoint locks provide superior thermal performance and peace of mind. For Hamden families looking to sell, updated windows and doors consistently rank among the improvements that most impress buyers.
