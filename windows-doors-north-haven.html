Why North Haven Homes Benefit from New Windows and Doors

If your North Haven home still has its original windows from the 1960s or 1970s, you're paying significantly more for heating and cooling than you need to. Single-pane glass and deteriorated weatherstripping allow conditioned air to escape and outside temperatures to infiltrate, making your HVAC system work overtime. The U.S. Department of Energy reports that heat gain and loss through inefficient windows accounts for 25-30% of residential energy use.

Modern replacement windows with double-pane insulated glass, Low-E coatings, and argon gas fill create a thermal barrier that keeps your home comfortable year-round. North Haven homeowners who upgrade consistently report noticeable improvements in comfort—no more cold drafts near windows in winter and less heat gain during summer—along with meaningful reductions in their utility bills.

Updated windows and a new front door also strengthen your home's position in the market. North Haven's desirable location between New Haven and Hartford, with easy access to I-91, makes it a competitive area for real estate. Buyers notice windows and doors immediately, and modern replacements signal a well-maintained home, often leading to faster sales and stronger offers.
